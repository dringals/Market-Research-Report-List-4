<p><p>Liquid level switches come in two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact types use physical contact to detect the liquid level and often consist of floats or probes that make direct contact with the liquid. Non-contact types, on the other hand, use technologies such as ultrasonic, capacitance, or optical sensors to detect the level without touching the liquid. Both types have their own advantages and are used in various industries based on the specific requirements of the application.</p></p>
