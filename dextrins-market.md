<p><p>Dextrins are a type of carbohydrate derived from starch, commonly found in maltodextrin and cyclodextrin forms. Maltodextrin is widely used in food and beverage industries as a thickener, stabilizer, or filler due to its neutral taste and ability to easily dissolve in liquids. Cyclodextrin, on the other hand, is known for its ability to encapsulate molecules, making it valuable in pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and food industries for improving stability and solubility of ingredients. Both types of dextrins cater to different market needs and applications.</p></p>
