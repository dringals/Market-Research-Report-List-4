<p><p>Endoscopic mucosal resection devices are commonly used in gastrointestinal endoscopy procedures to remove abnormal or precancerous tissues in the mucosal layer of the digestive tract. There are three main types of devices used for this purpose:</p><p>1. Entry Needle: A thin, flexible needle used to access and inject fluid or lift the target tissue for easier resection.</p><p>2. Mucosectomy Snare: A wire loop or snare used to capture and remove the abnormal tissue.</p><p>3. Probe: A specialized instrument used for precise visualization and sampling of the mucosal layer before resection. Each of these devices plays a crucial role in the success of endoscopic mucosal resection procedures.</p></p>
