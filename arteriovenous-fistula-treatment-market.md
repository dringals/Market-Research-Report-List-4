Dural Arteriovenous Fistulas occur within the dura mater of the brain and are often treated through endovascular procedures. Peripheral Arteriovenous Fistulas involve abnormal connections between arteries and veins in the limbs and are commonly treated with surgery. Pial or Cerebral Arteriovenous Fistulas are abnormalities within the brain tissue and may require a combination of surgical and endovascular treatments for resolution.</p></p>
