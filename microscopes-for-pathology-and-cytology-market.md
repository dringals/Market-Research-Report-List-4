<p><p>Microscopes used in pathology and cytology are typically categorized into two main types: monocular and binocular. Monocular microscopes have a single eyepiece, making them more compact and affordable, but they may cause eye strain during prolonged use. Binocular microscopes have two eyepieces which provide a more comfortable viewing experience and allow for better depth perception. They are generally preferred in professional settings where precise observation is crucial. Both types offer various magnification levels and illumination options to suit different laboratory needs.</p></p>
