<p><p>3D biological printing market includes inkjet-based bioprinting, which uses droplets of bio-ink to build tissues layer by layer. Syringe-based bioprinting involves using a syringe to deposit bio-ink onto a substrate, allowing for precise control over the placement of cells and materials. Laser-based bioprinting uses lasers to solidify bio-inks and create intricate structures with high resolution. Each type of bioprinting has its own advantages and applications in the field of tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.</p></p>
