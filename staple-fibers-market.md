<p><p>Staple fibers can be divided into three main market types: cotton, cellulosic, and polyester. Cotton fibers are derived from the cotton plant and are natural, soft, and breathable making them popular for clothing and textiles. Cellulosic fibers are made from cellulose extracted from plants such as bamboo, and offer properties similar to cotton. Polyester fibers are synthetic and known for their durability, wrinkle resistance, and ability to retain shape, making them widely used in a variety of applications.</p></p>
