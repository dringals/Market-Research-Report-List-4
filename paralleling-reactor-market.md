<p><ul><li>Oil-Immersed</li><li>Air-Core</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The paralleling reactor market consists of two main types: oil-immersed and air-core reactors. Oil-immersed reactors use mineral oil for insulation and cooling, making them suitable for high power applications. They are commonly used in industrial settings and power grids. On the other hand, air-core reactors do not use oil insulation, making them lighter and more compact. They are typically used in high-frequency applications like renewable energy systems and power electronics. Both types play a crucial role in managing power flow and voltage regulation in electrical systems.</p></p>
