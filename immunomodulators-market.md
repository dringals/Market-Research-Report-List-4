<p><p>Immunomodulators are substances that can modify or regulate the immune system. Immunosuppressants are a type of immunomodulator that suppress the immune response, commonly used in autoimmune diseases and organ transplant patients. On the other hand, immunostimulants are another type of immunomodulator that enhance the immune response, often used to boost immunity in individuals with weakened immune systems. Both types of immunomodulators play a crucial role in maintaining immune system balance and treating various immune-related disorders.</p></p>
