<p><p>Wireless Power Controller ICs are divided into two main types: Transmitter ICs and Receiver ICs. Transmitter ICs are responsible for generating and transmitting power wirelessly to devices utilizing technologies such as magnetic resonance or inductive coupling. Receiver ICs, on the other hand, receive the power transmitted wirelessly and convert it into usable electricity for charging or powering devices. Both types of ICs play crucial roles in enabling wireless power transfer technologies in various applications across industries.</p></p>
