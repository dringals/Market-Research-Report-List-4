<p><p>Reinforced carbon-carbon (RCC) materials are used in high-temperature applications due to their excellent thermal and mechanical properties. Two common market types for RCC production are Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. In the CVD method, carbon is deposited onto a structure in a gaseous form. On the other hand, the Liquid Impregnation Process involves impregnating the structure with liquid carbon. Both methods result in highly durable and heat-resistant RCC materials that are utilized in aerospace, automotive, and other industries.</p></p>
