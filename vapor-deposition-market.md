<p><p>Vapor deposition refers to a process where a material is deposited onto a solid surface in the form of vapor. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) involves the chemical reaction of gases to form a thin film on a substrate, while Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) uses physical processes such as evaporation or sputtering to deposit material. Both CVD and PVD have various applications in industries such as semiconductors, optics, and coatings. These processes are crucial for producing high-quality thin films with specific properties.</p></p>
