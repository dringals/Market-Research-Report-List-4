<p><ul><li>Negative Thermosensitive Hydrogel</li><li>Positive Thermosensitive Hydrogel</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Thermosensitive hydrogels can be categorized into two main types: negative thermosensitive hydrogels and positive thermosensitive hydrogels. Negative thermosensitive hydrogels exhibit a decrease in volume upon heating, while positive thermosensitive hydrogels show an increase in volume with temperature rise. Negative thermosensitive hydrogels are commonly used for drug delivery applications as they can release drugs in response to temperature changes, while positive thermosensitive hydrogels are utilized for tissue engineering and cell encapsulation due to their ability to expand and contract in response to temperature fluctuations.</p></p>
